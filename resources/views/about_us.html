Introduction to Cryptocurrencies

Cryptocurrencies are digital assets protected by cryptography and existing exclusively in virtual space, unlike familiar fiat currencies like the US dollar. The most well-known cryptocurrencies - Bitcoin (BTC), Ethereum (ETH), and Solana (SOL) - operate on blockchain technology, which is a decentralized digital ledger.

Each transaction is permanently recorded in the blockchain after verification and confirmation. Unlike traditional currencies, control over which is concentrated in the hands of banks and financial regulators, cryptocurrency transactions are processed by a decentralized network of participants.

Depending on the consensus mechanism, any user can participate in confirming transactions and adding them to the blockchain. It is this open architecture that makes cryptocurrencies truly decentralized. Many experts believe that blockchain can radically change the global financial system - and the growth of corporate investments from major companies, including Samsung, BlackRock, Morgan Stanley, and Alphabet, only strengthens this trend.

Trading cryptocurrencies provides access to the world of decentralized finance and allows you to touch modern technological solutions.
